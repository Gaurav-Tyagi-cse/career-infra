Scholarships in Malaysia

The government of Malaysia and private institutions offer Scholarships in Malaysia for international students.

  1. Malaysia International Scholarship (MIS)

    The Malaysian International Scholarship (MIS) is awarded by the Malaysian government to international students with outstanding academic records, leadership skills, and a commitment to contribute to their home country post-study.

  2. Malaysian Technical Cooperation Programme (MTCP)

    The MTCP Scholarship, provided by the Malaysian government, supports international students from developing countries in pursuing higher education in Malaysia. It aims to foster technical cooperation and human resource development. The scholarship covers tuition fees, living expenses, and round-trip airfare to Malaysia.

  3. ASEAN Scholarships for Postgraduate Studies

    The ASEAN Scholarships for Postgraduate Studies are available to citizens of ASEAN member countries pursuing postgraduate programs in fellow ASEAN countries. It covers tuition, living expenses, and airfare. Available for various subjects, including Science, Social Sciences, and Humanities. Duration varies: up to two years for Master's and up to three years for Doctoral programs.

  4. Malaysian international scholarships for postgraduate students

    Talented international students with excellent academic records and outstanding co-curricular backgrounds are welcome to apply for these scholarships and further their studies at any selected and well-established Malaysian public and selected private universities at the postgraduate or postdoctoral level.

Documents required to study in Malaysia

To study in Malaysia, international students typically need to prepare and submit several documents for admission and visa purposes. The exact requirements may vary depending on the institution and the specific program of study. However, common documents required to study in Malaysia include:

  • Letter of Acceptance
  • Academic Transcripts and Certificates
  • English-language proficiency
  • Health examination report
  • Financial documents
  • Visa application form
  • Study plan or Statement of Purpose
  • Two Passport-size photographs
  • Additional documents, if required

Malaysia Study Visa Process

All applications for a Malaysian student visa for international students are arranged through Malaysian educational institutions. The next-stage of processing, approval, and issuance of student passes and visas is carried out by the immigration department in Malaysia. Applications will be submitted to the educational institutions to the directors of the pass and permit division, Malaysia.

The prospective student will be informed of their application status by the immigration department of Malaysia through their educational institutions. This information is imparted within 7 days of application, regardless of whether the application has been successful or not. The educational institutions that enrol the students are responsible for making the necessary arrangements pertaining to immigration matters.


  • Two passport-size photographs of the applicant.
  • Two photocopies of the student’s passport or travel document
  • The educational institution is required to sign a “Personal Bond” on behalf of the student. No fee is charged for a Personal Bond.
  • Student Pass application form (IMM14) in duplicate
  • An Offer Letter or letter of acceptance from the educational institution to the student

FAQs

  • What are the documents required to study in Malaysia?

    In order to gain admission to a Malaysian university, there are certain documents that need to be submitted by the students. The documents include a passport, letter of acceptance, academic transcripts and certificates, English language proficiency report, a health examination report, financial documents, a visa application form, a study plan or statement of purpose, passport-sized photographs, and additional documents if required.

  • What is the cost of studying a Master’s degree in Malaysia?

    The tuition fees for a Master’s degree in Malaysia range from MYR 4,000 to 15,000 per year.

  • What scholarships are available for international students to study in Malaysia?

    Several scholarships are available for international students in Malaysia, including the Malaysia International Scholarship (MIS), the Malaysian Technical Cooperation Programme (MTCP), the Malaysian International Scholarships for Postgraduate students, and the ASEAN Scholarships for Postgraduate students.

  • What is the quality of education in Malaysia?

    Malaysia offers high-quality education with internationally recognized programs. Its universities feature modern facilities and experienced faculty, making it a great choice for international students.

  • What are the popular courses offered by Malaysian universities?
